The structure of the Earth ...
-> Crust : outer layer, solid rocks ( Deepest bore hole, Kola Peninsula in Russia with 12 km did not reach the ground)
-> Lithosphere : crust + upper part of the Upper Mantle. Solid. Consists of different plates which are in constant motion. Around 100 km thin.
-> Upper Mantle : molten rock ( magma)
-> Lower Mantle : solid rock
-> Outer Core : molten material, mainly iron very hot.
-> Inner Core : solid material beacause of the immense pressure), mainly iron. Hottest spot - 5500°C

Plate Tectonics
-> German scientist ALfred Wegener defined continental drift theory ( Kontinentalsdriftstheorie)
-> The Theory of Plate Tectonics tries to give an answer to that
-> The earths crust is divided into pieces - the plates.
-> They move because of convection currents ( Konvektionsströme )
-> Those currents occur because warm material (from near the Lower Mantle) rises and cool material(from near the crust) sinks
-> Two different types of crust
- the continental crust
- the oceanic crust

Earthquakes
-> The scales :
Mercalli Scale & Richter Scale
-> Mercalli Scale:
- 12 Grades
-Describes the imapct of the earthquake at a special location.
-The grade is dependant on population density, building technique or distance to the epicentre.
-> Richter Scale:
- Unlimited Grades
- Shows the intesity with help of a seismograph.
- Scientists call the grades of this scale magnitude - which always means the intensity at the epicentere. (e.g: grade 4 can cause little damage on buildings)

The plates move in three different ways:
- Divergent ( Plates move away from eachother. Magma rises to the surface , new crust appeares ; Gentle volcanic ; Mid Atlantic ridge)
- Conservative (Two plates move sideways past eachother ; Earthquakes ; Pacific an Notheren American Plates)
- Convergent ( two plates collide ; Earthquakes , Violent volcanic , Folded mountains ; Ring of Fire)

Different types of Volcanoes

Shield Volcanoes
- Flat Structure
- Lava is very hot ( approx. 1200°C) and liquid.
- Lava flows quicly -- therefore the volcano is rather flat.
- Few Gases -- not very explosive
E.g.: Hawaiian islands

Strato Volcanoes
- Formed like a cone (Kegel).
- Lava is quite cool( approx. 800°C) and viscous
- Lava folws slowly - cools and build a new layer of Lava and ashes - volcano grows higher
- A lot of gases - very explosive
e.g.: Vesuvius

What is a Calderas?
Volcanoes can create calderas when parts of the volcano collapse. Those holes can be filled with water (e.g.: Island Santorin)
